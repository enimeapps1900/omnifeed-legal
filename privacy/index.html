Privacy Policy

Last updated: 14 May 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how AF APPS ("we", "us", "our") handles information when you use the Omnifeed mobile application ("the App"). By using the App you agree to this Policy.

1. Who we are

AF APPS, an independent app developer. Contact: lflifli7@gmail.com.

2. The short version

  • Omnifeed has no account system and no server that stores your content. Your connected accounts, sign-in tokens, and drafts live only on your device.
  • We never see or store your social-network passwords.
  • We collect anonymous diagnostics (crash reports, basic usage analytics) to keep the App working, and we show ads in the free version.
  • We do not sell your personal information.

3. Information stored on your device

The following never leaves your device except to talk directly to the social network it belongs to:

  • Connected-account data. When you connect a social network (e.g. Reddit, Bluesky, Mastodon), the App stores the access token issued by that network, plus your public handle and avatar URL, in encrypted device storage (Android Keystore).
  • Sign-in credentials. For networks that use a system browser or in-app web sign-in, you authenticate on the network's own page — we never receive those credentials. For Bluesky, the app password you enter is sent directly to Bluesky to obtain a token and is not stored by the App.
  • Drafts and app settings. Post drafts and your preferences (theme, etc.) are saved locally.

Disconnecting an account, or uninstalling the App, removes this data from your device.

4. Information we collect automatically

To keep the App stable and understand which features are used, we collect a limited amount of anonymous diagnostic data through Google Firebase (Analytics and Crashlytics):

  • Device & app data — device model, OS version, language, app version, and an anonymous installation identifier.
  • Crash reports — stack traces and a short trail of in-app actions leading up to a crash. We do not include the text you type or the content of your feeds.
  • Usage analytics — which screens are opened and which core actions occur (for example: an account was connected, a post was published). These events are not tied to your identity.

5. Information from the networks you connect

When you connect a social network, that network returns your basic public profile (such as your username and avatar) and the feed content you ask the App to display. This is processed on your device to show you your feeds. We request only what is needed for the App to function.

6. What we do NOT do

  • We do not collect your social-network passwords.
  • We do not read or upload your contacts, SMS, call logs, photos, or precise location.
  • We do not run a server that stores your posts, drafts, or feeds.
  • We do not sell your personal information.
  • We do not use your social-network content to train AI models.

7. How we use information

  • To provide the App's core features — showing your connected feeds and publishing posts you create.
  • To diagnose crashes and improve stability and performance.
  • To understand, in aggregate, which features are used.
  • To display advertising in the free version of the App.
  • To respond to your support requests.

8. Advertising

  • The free version of the App displays ads through Google AdMob. AdMob may use a device advertising identifier to serve and measure ads.
  • For users in the EEA, the UK, and Switzerland, we use Google's User Messaging Platform to request consent for personalized advertising before any such ads are shown.
  • You can reset or limit your advertising identifier at any time in your device's settings.

9. Who we share information with

  • Google Firebase — anonymous crash reporting and analytics.
  • Google AdMob — to serve ads in the free version.
  • The social networks you connect — the App communicates directly with each network's API so you can read and post.
  • Legal authorities — only where required by law.

We do not share your information with anyone else.

10. Data security

  • Account tokens are stored encrypted on your device using the Android Keystore system.
  • All network communication uses HTTPS (TLS).
  • Because tokens and content stay on your device, there is no central database of user data to breach.

11. Data retention

  • On-device data (tokens, drafts, settings) — kept until you disconnect the account, clear it in the App, or uninstall the App.
  • Anonymous analytics — retained by Firebase for up to 14 months.
  • Crash reports — retained for up to 90 days.

12. Your rights

Because your personal content stays on your device, you are in direct control of it. You can:

  • Delete your data — disconnect accounts in Settings → Privacy, or uninstall the App. You can also submit a request at our data-deletion page or by email.
  • Opt out of personalized ads via your device settings and the in-app consent controls.
  • Contact us with any privacy question.

Residents of the EEA/UK (GDPR) and California (CCPA/CPRA) have additional rights; contact us to exercise them.

13. Children

Omnifeed is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them. If you believe a child has provided us information, contact us and we will remove it.

14. International users

Our diagnostic and advertising providers (Google) may process data in the United States and other countries. Google applies standard contractual safeguards for these transfers.

15. Changes to this Policy

We will update the "Last updated" date above when this Policy changes, and for significant changes we will notify you in the App. Continued use of the App after a change means you accept the updated Policy.

16. Contact us

Questions about this Policy or your data: lflifli7@gmail.com.
